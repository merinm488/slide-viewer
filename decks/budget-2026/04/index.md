# Revenue Sources

The government generates revenue through various channels to fund expenditures and support public services.

**Tax Revenue (60% of Total Revenue)**

| Source | Share | Key Changes in 2026 |
|--------|-------|---------------------|
| Direct Taxes | 35% | New tax regime, higher exemption limit |
| Indirect Taxes | 25% | GST rationalization, fewer slabs |
| Corporate Tax | 20% | Incentives for manufacturing & startups |

**Non-Tax Revenue (25% of Total Revenue)**

- **Interest Income**: Earnings from loans given to states and public sector enterprises
- **Dividends**: Profits from RBI and public sector companies
- **Fees & Fines**: Regulatory fees, court fees, and various penalties

**Other Sources (15% of Total Revenue)**

- **Disinvestment**: Proceeds from selling government stake in PSUs
- **Borrowing**: Market loans and external assistance
- **Grants-in-Aid**: Central grants to state governments

**New Revenue Initiatives**

- Expanded tax base through improved compliance
- Simplified GST return filing system
- Digital services tax on large tech companies
