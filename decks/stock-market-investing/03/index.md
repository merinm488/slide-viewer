# Types of Stocks

Understanding different stock categories helps you build a diversified portfolio that matches your goals and risk tolerance.

## By Company Size

| Type | Market Cap | Characteristics |
|------|------------|----------------|
| **Large-cap** | $10B+ | Stable, established companies, lower risk |
| **Mid-cap** | $2B-$10B | Balance of growth and stability |
| **Small-cap** | $300M-$2B | Higher growth potential, more volatile |

## By Investment Style

- **Growth stocks**: Companies expected to grow faster than the market
  - Typically don't pay dividends
  - Reinvest profits into expansion
  - Higher risk, higher potential reward

- **Value stocks**: Stocks trading below their intrinsic value
  - Often established companies
  - May pay dividends
  - Potential for price appreciation when market recognizes value

- **Dividend stocks**: Companies that pay regular dividends
  - Provide steady income
  - Often mature, stable companies
  - Good for income-focused investors
