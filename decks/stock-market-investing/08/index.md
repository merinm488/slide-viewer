# Common Investing Strategies

Different investors use different approaches based on their goals, personality, and available time. Here are some popular strategies to consider.

## Long-Term Buy and Hold

Buy quality stocks and hold them for years or decades.

**Advantages**
- Minimal time commitment
- Lower transaction costs and taxes
- Historically strong returns
- Less stress from daily volatility

**Best for**: Most investors, especially beginners

## Dollar-Cost Averaging

Invest a fixed amount regularly regardless of stock price.

Example: Invest $500 every month in an index fund

**Advantages**
- Removes emotional timing decisions
- Buy more shares when prices are low
- Reduces impact of market volatility
- Builds investing discipline

## Index Fund Investing

Buy funds that track market indices like the S&P 500.

**Advantages**
- Instant diversification
- Low fees (expense ratios)
- No need to pick individual stocks
- Beats most active managers over time

## Dividend Investing

Focus on stocks that pay regular dividends.

**Advantages**
- Regular income stream
- Often stable, mature companies
- Dividends can be reinvested for compound growth

## Value Investing

Buy undervalued stocks trading below their intrinsic worth.

Made famous by Warren Buffett, this strategy requires analyzing company fundamentals and patience.
