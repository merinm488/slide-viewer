# Hip Hop Culture

## From Bronx to the World

| Milestone | Year | Significance |
|-----------|------|-------------|
| **Born** | 1973 | 1520 Sedgwick Avenue, Bronx block party |
| **First hit** | 1979 | "Rapper's Delight" - Sugarhill Gang |
| **Golden Age** | 1986-1997 | Rakim, Public Enemy, N.W.A, Tupac, Biggie |
| **Mainstream** | 2000s | Eminem, Jay-Z, OutKast cross over |
| **Streaming era** | 2010s+ | Drake, Kendrick, J. Cole dominate |

## The Five Elements of Hip Hop

| Element | Description | Pioneers |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| **MCing** | Rapping, lyrical delivery | Kool Herc, Coke La Rock |
| **DJing** | Turntablism, mixing, scratching | Grandmaster Flash, DJ Kool Herc |
| **Breakdancing** | B-boying, acrobatic dance | Rock Steady Crew |
| **Graffiti** | Visual art, spray painting | Keith Haring, Jean-Michel Basquiat |
| **Knowledge** | Consciousness, social awareness | Afrika Bambaataa |

## Hip Hop Evolution by Era

### Old School (1973-1986)
- **Sugarhill Gang** - "Rapper's Delight"
- **Run-D.M.C.** - First gold/ platinum rap group
- **Grandmaster Flash** - "The Message" - social commentary
- Simple beats, party anthems, disco-influenced

### Golden Age (1986-1997)
- **Public Enemy** - Political hip hop, Bomb Squad production
- **N.W.A** - Gangsta rap emerges from West Coast
- **Rakim** - Lyrical complexity, internal rhymes
- **Tupac Shakur** - Poetry, social commentary
- **The Notorious B.I.G.** - Flow, storytelling
- **Wu-Tang Clan** - Collective, kung-fu samples

### Modern Era (2000s-Present)
| Artist | Style | Impact |
|--------|-------|--------|
| **Eminem** | Technical skill, storytelling | Best-selling rapper |
| **Jay-Z** | Business acumen, mafioso rap | 500M+ records sold |
| **Kendrick Lamar** | Concept albums, jazz influence | Pulitzer Prize winner |
| **Drake** | Melodic rap, R&B fusion | Most streamed artist |
| **J. Cole** | Lyrical, self-produced | No features needed |
